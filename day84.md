## 🌤️ Afternoon Block (2 hours): Job Search Knowledge Quiz

### 15 Questions

**Q1.** What does ATS stand for, and why does it matter?

**Q2.** How long should a fresh grad resume be?

**Q3.** What file format should your resume be in?

**Q4.** Name 3 job platforms for Singapore.

**Q5.** What's the STAR method for behavioral interviews?

**Q6.** How many LinkedIn skills should you list (minimum)?

**Q7.** What should your LinkedIn headline contain?

**Q8.** What's the ideal length for a cover letter?

**Q9.** How many applications per week is a good target?

**Q10.** Why are referrals more effective than cold applications?

**Q11.** What should you do within 24 hours of an interview?

**Q12.** Name 3 common data engineering interview formats.

**Q13.** What's the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 3 companies in your application strategy?

**Q14.** How long should your project pitch be when explaining to a hiring manager?

**Q15.** What's the #1 thing employers test for in fresh grad DE interviews?

<details>
<summary>🔑 Answers</summary>

**A1:** ATS = Applicant Tracking System. It's software that scans resumes for keywords before a human sees them. ~75% of resumes get rejected by ATS.

**A2:** 1 page. Strictly. No exceptions for fresh grads.

**A3:** PDF. Always. Word docs can look different on different systems.

**A4:** MyCareersFuture, LinkedIn Jobs, NodeFlair, TechInAsia, Indeed.

**A5:** Situation, Task, Action, Result — a framework for structuring behavioral interview answers.

**A6:** At least 10-15, all relevant to data engineering.

**A7:** Your target role + top skills + differentiator. E.g., "Data Engineer | SQL · Python · AWS · dbt"

**A8:** 300-400 words (3 short paragraphs).

**A9:** 15-20 per week (quality applications, not spam).

**A10:** Referrals are 5-10x more likely to result in an interview because someone inside the company vouches for you.

**A11:** Send a thank-you email. Mention something specific from the conversation.

**A12:** SQL live coding, Python/ETL coding, system design, behavioral, take-home assignment.

**A13:** Tier 1 = dream companies (extra effort, custom application). Tier 3 = broad net (efficient, standard application).

**A14:** ~90 seconds (the "Level 2" pitch).

**A15:** SQL. Almost every DE interview starts with SQL live coding. If you can't write complex SQL queries, nothing else matters.
</details>
