## 🌤️ Afternoon Block (2 hours): Referral Strategy + Application Workflow

### How to Get Referrals

Referrals are 5-10x more likely to get an interview than cold applications.

**Step 1:** Find employees on LinkedIn
- Search: "[Company] data engineer" 
- Filter: 2nd-degree connections
- Look for: alumni from your university, similar background

**Step 2:** Send a message
```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you work as a Data Engineer at [Company]. I'm currently 
preparing for DE roles in Singapore and have been building my portfolio 
with SQL, Python, AWS, and Airflow.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute chat about your experience at 
[Company]? I'd love to learn what the team looks for in junior candidates.

No pressure at all — I appreciate any insights!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Step 3:** After the chat (if it goes well)
```
Hi [Name], 

Thank you so much for your time! Your insights about [Company]'s data 
stack were really helpful.

I noticed [Company] has an opening for [Role]. Would you be comfortable 
referring me? I've attached my resume for reference.

Either way, thank you again for your time!
```

### Application Workflow (per application)

```
1. Read job description carefully (5 min)
2. Highlight matching keywords (2 min)
3. Tailor resume if needed (5 min)
4. Write customized cover letter (10 min)
5. Check for referral opportunities (3 min)
6. Apply (5 min)
7. Log in tracking spreadsheet (2 min)
8. Set follow-up reminder (1 min)
Total: ~30 minutes
```

### Daily Application Goal

- **Weekdays:** 2-3 applications per day (quality over quantity)
- **Weekends:** 5 applications + research next week's targets
- **Target:** 15-20 applications per week

## 🌙 Evening (1 hour): Elevator Pitch + Follow-up Strategy

### Your 30-Second Elevator Pitch

```
"Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm an aspiring Data Engineer based in Singapore. 
I've spent the last 3 months building a portfolio of data projects — 
including a serverless AWS pipeline and a star schema data warehouse. 
I'm skilled in SQL, Python, AWS, dbt, and Airflow, and I'm looking 
for a junior role where I can build production data pipelines."
```

Practice saying this 5 times until it sounds natural.

### Follow-up Rules

- **After applying:** Wait 1 week, then send a brief follow-up on LinkedIn
- **After interview:** Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- **After no response:** Follow up once more after 2 weeks, then move on
- **After rejection:** Reply politely, ask for feedback, stay in touch
